Le Comte de Monte-Cristo
  • Le Comte de Monte-Cristo
    • The novel depicts a tale of retribution and revenge, set in France during two major periods, the Bourbon and July dynasties. The first mate of the Pharaoh's ship, Dantes, is entrusted by the captain's dying request to deliver a letter for Napoleon. He is framed by two villains who are jealous of him, and is sent to death row, where his cellmate, Father Faria, teaches him all sorts of knowledge, and also tells him the secret of a batch of treasures on his deathbed. He managed to escape from prison and found the treasure, and became very rich. From then on he assumed the name of the Count of Monte Cristo, and after careful planning, he repaid his benefactors and punished three enemies who were bent on killing him.
